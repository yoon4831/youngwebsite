Traditional Clothes In Korea

Korea's traditional attire is called "Hanbok" and it symbolizes the beauty of Korean tradition.Worn daily up until just 100 years ago, hanbok comes in various shapes and colors, reflecting the culture and lifestyle of the its time. Nowadays, it is only worn on special occasions or anniversaries. It is a formal wear and many Koreans keep a hanbok for such occasions.

Hanbok consists of top, bottom and underclothing.

The basic upper garment of the hanbok is called the Jeogori.



The bottom part of men's hanbok is called Baji, which has the meaning of pants in Korean.

The bottom part of women's hanbok is called Chima, which has the meaning of skirt in Korean.
Hanboks are made in different shapes, colors, and patterns.
