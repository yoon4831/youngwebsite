Traditional Food in Korea

Kimchi(김치) is a Korean traditional side dish of salted and fermented vegetables, such as napa cabbage and korean radish, made with a widely varying selection of seasonings including gochugaru (chili powder), spring opinions, garlic and ginger.


Bulgogi(불고기) is a Korean-style grilled dish made of thin, marinated slices of beef or pork grilled on a barbeque or on a stove-top griddle. It is also often stir-fried in a pan in home cooking.


Bibimbap(비빔밥) is a korean rice dish. The term "bibim" means mixing various ingredients, and the "bap" refers to rice. Bibimbap is served as a bowl of warm white rice topped with seasoned vegetables called namul or kimchi and gochujang(chili pepper paste), and soy sauce, or fermented soybean paste.


Japchae(잡채) is a stir-fried starch noodles with beef and vegetables.
